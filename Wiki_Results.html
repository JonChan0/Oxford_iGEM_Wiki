Functionality of ΦCD27L Endolysin

The CD27L endolysin is a critical component of our system, as it acts as the agent of C. difficile lysis. Three parts were tested throughout this sub-section of project—they are summarised for clarity in Table 1.


Table 1: Different Constructs of CD27L Endolysin in pET28A Expression Vector

Construct Name Construct Part Number
Dundee-CD27L Hcp-HA_Tag- CD27L1-179 BBa_K895004
CD27L 6His-SpyTag-CD27L BBa_K3183200
CD27L1-179 6His-SpyTag-CD27L1-179 BBa_K3183201

We wanted to optimise the killing efficacy of the endolysin. Thus, we sought out the endolysin in an existing part made by the 2012 iGEM Team Dundee. This part (Dundee-CD27L1-179) was comprised of CD27L1-179 endolysin linked to the hcp Type VI secretion tag via an haemagglutinin (HA) epitope tag (BBa_K895004).

After multiple attempts at expression, we were unable to express the protein, with further information on the Part Improvement page. Fig. 1 provides a comparison between expression of the full-length endolysin, CD27L and the 2012 Dundee iGEM part BBa_K895004.

Fig 1: SDS-PAGE Gel Comparing CD27L Endolysin Samples with Dundee-CD27L Samples

Regardless, expression of the full-length CD27L expression was successful as shown in Fig.1 and this was quantitatively verified via mass spectrometry as shown in Fig. 2.

Fig. 2: Mass Spectrometry of CD27L Endolysin Following Ni-NTA Purification

Given this outcome, we decided to express only the truncated CD27L1-179 endolysin, removing the hcp Type VI secretion tag which we assumed to have interfered with expression. In contrast, successful expression occurred for this new part BBa_K31830012, as shown in Figure 3.

Due to safety concerns, our endolysin killing assays could only be carried out on on Bacillus subitlis, a Category 1 bacteria as opposed to Clostridium difficile, a Category 2 bacteria. B. subtilis has a similar peptidoglycan layer to C. difficile, allowing us to quantify killing data.
