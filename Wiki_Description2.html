The problem now

C. difficile infection (CDI) is the leading cause of hospital and nursing home acquired infection in the developed world. Today, nearly half of all hospitalised patients are prescribed antibiotics. These antibiotics drastically alter the gut flora, killing both beneficial and pathogenic bacteria alike. Such a shift in the dynamics of the gut microbiome allows opportunistic bacteria like C. difficile to propagate. The resulting toxins produced by C. difficile toxins lead to extremely unpleasant symptoms such as diarrhoea, bowel perforation and severe dehydration. Presently, patients over the age of 65 account for more than 80% of the deaths from CDI. As such, our ever-aging populations coupled with the inevitable rise of antibiotic resistance makes C. difficile a major threat.
